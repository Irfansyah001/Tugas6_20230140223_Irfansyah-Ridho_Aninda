What's Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is a digital currency that allows people to send or receive money over the internet. It operates without a central authority like a bank or government. Transactions are verified by a network of computers (called nodes) and recorded in a public ledger called the blockchain.

Bitcoin Explained

Bitcoin (BTC) is a cryptocurrency (a virtual currency) designed to act as money and a form of payment outside the control of any one person, group, or entity. This removes the need for trusted third-party involvement (e.g., a mint or bank) in financial transactions.

How Cryptocurrency Works

Cryptocurrencies are digital or virtual currencies secured by cryptography and operate on decentralized networks, typically using blockchain technology. They are not issued by any central bank or government and are not tangible physical assets. Instead, they exist as digital entries on a public ledger, allowing peer-to-peer transactions without a middleman.
